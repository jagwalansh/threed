# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want threeD to be a welcoming and respectful project for everyone interested in 3D websites, creative tools, frontend engineering, design, and open source collaboration.

As contributors and maintainers, we pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, or technology background.

## Expected behavior

Examples of behavior that helps create a positive community include:

- Being respectful and patient with others.
- Giving constructive feedback.
- Assuming good intent while still addressing harm when it happens.
- Welcoming beginners and people learning new tools.
- Keeping discussions focused on improving the project.
- Accepting that people may have different opinions, workflows, and levels of experience.

## Unacceptable behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or threats.
- Insults, personal attacks, or demeaning comments.
- Discriminatory language or behavior.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or attention.
- Trolling or repeated disruptive behavior.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Dismissing people because they are new or still learning.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, commit messages, and any other public or private communication related to threeD.

It also applies when someone is representing the project in public spaces, such as social media, events, or community discussions.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing acceptable behavior. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull requests, or other contributions that do not follow this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may also temporarily or permanently ban contributors for behavior that is harmful, inappropriate, or disruptive.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to the project maintainer. If this project has a public maintainer email or security contact, use that channel. Otherwise, open a private communication channel with the repository owner where possible.

Reports will be reviewed as carefully and respectfully as possible. The privacy and safety of the person reporting will be treated seriously.
